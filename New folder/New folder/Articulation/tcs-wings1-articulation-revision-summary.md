### Scenario 1: Intercultural Silence
**Q: Your colleague from Japan is silent during a meeting when asked for input. What should you infer?**[cite: 1]
* A) They agree with you[cite: 1]
* B) They didn't hear the question[cite: 1]
* **C) They are likely uncomfortable or disagree silently**[cite: 1]
* D) They have nothing to say[cite: 1]
* **Answer: C**[cite: 1]
* **Explanation**: In Japanese culture, silence often indicates discomfort or disagreement, not necessarily agreement[cite: 1].

### Scenario 2: Agile Time-Boxing
**Q: In a daily stand-up, one member keeps exceeding their time. What is the best response?**[cite: 1]
* A) Let them finish every time[cite: 1]
* B) Cut them off rudely[cite: 1]
* **C) Politely remind them of the time-box and take it offline**[cite: 1]
* D) Ignore and continue[cite: 1]
* **Answer: C**[cite: 1]
* **Explanation**: Scrum meetings are time-boxed. Issues should be discussed after the meeting if they exceed the limit[cite: 1].
