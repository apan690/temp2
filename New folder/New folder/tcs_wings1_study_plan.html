07 8 Strategies to Guarantee Distinction

01
Mine Your Nov '25 Experience First
Spend Week 1 rethinking what actually cost you marks last time — time management, a specific topic cluster, or question format. Target those ruthlessly first. Don't re-learn what you already know.
02
Use iEvolve as Your North Star
Wings 1 MCQs are largely aligned with iEvolve course content. If iEvolve covers a topic, it will appear in the exam. Complete every module and score 80%+ on iEvolve quizzes as your benchmark.
03
The "Transformation Sheet" for Informatica
Create a one-page reference: Transformation name → Active/Passive → Connected/Unconnected → Key use case → Common MCQ trap. Review it every Monday. This alone can win you 5–7 Tech MCQ marks.
04
Tableau: Build, Don't Just Watch
For MCQs: knowing LOD syntax cold is non-negotiable. For hands-on: build dashboards daily, not just exercises. The exam gives you a business scenario — you need muscle memory, not recall.
05
BizzSkill: Learn the TCS Lens
Questions are often "what would TCS expect you to do?" — not generic business knowledge. Always pick the answer that shows professional escalation, teamwork, and ethics above individual action. Know TCS POSH and DEI policies cold.
06
Articulation: Speed + Elimination
You likely know most answers instinctively — but time pressure causes errors. Practice eliminating 2 options in under 10 seconds. For comprehension, read the questions first, then the passage. This saves 30–40% reading time.
07
Weekly "Error Logbook"
Every wrong answer in every mock goes into a logbook with: (a) the correct answer, (b) why you got it wrong, (c) the concept it tests. Review this log every Sunday. The same question types repeat.
08
Simulate Exam Conditions from Week 6
Use exact time limits. No phone. No pausing. Sit at a desk. Log into a browser as if it's real. Anxiety is the hidden enemy for returning candidates — simulate it away so the real exam feels familiar.
